Q: What is a savings account?
A: A savings account is a deposit account for individuals that earns interest and supports everyday payments and withdrawals.
Q: What is a current account?
A: A current account is a high transaction account for businesses with cheque/online facilities and typically low or no interest.
Q: What is a salary account?
A: A salary account is a zero balance variant opened by an employer for monthly credit of salary with regular savings features.
Q: What is a fixed deposit (FD) account?
A: An FD is a time deposit with a fixed tenure and interest rate, usually higher than savings accounts.
Q: What is a recurring deposit (RD) account?
A: An RD lets you deposit a fixed amount monthly for a chosen tenure to earn fixed interest.
Q: What is an NRE account?
A: Non Resident External account for NRIs to hold foreign income in INR; fully repatriable and interest is tax free in India.
Q: What is an NRO account?
A: Non Resident Ordinary account to manage income earned in India; interest is taxable and repatriation is limited.
Q: What is an FCNR account?
A: Foreign Currency Non Resident term deposit in foreign currency, shielding against INR currency risk; interest tax free in India.
Q: What is a Demat account?
A: A Demat holds securities like shares and bonds in electronic form for trading and investments.
Q: What is a BSBD/BSBDA account?
A: Basic Savings Bank Deposit Account offering zero balance essential banking features to promote inclusion.
Q: What is account opening KYC?
A: Know Your Customer checks: identity, address, PAN/Form 60, and photograph; eKYC/Video KYC may be available.
Q: Can I open a bank account online?
A: Yes, many banks support instant digital or video KYC savings accounts.
Q: What is minimum balance?
A: The bank defined minimum average balance for standard accounts; BSBD remains zero balance.
Q: What interest do savings accounts pay?
A: Bank set rates, often tiered by balance; check your banks live schedule for exact rates.
Q: How often is savings interest credited?
A: Typically quarterly or monthly as per bank policy; details are disclosed by each bank.
Q: What is account number vs customer ID?
A: Account number identifies a specific account; customer ID maps all relationships under one profile.
Q: What documents are needed for savings account opening?
A: Valid ID, address proof, PAN/Form 60, and photo; minor/senior variants may add specifics.
Q: Can students and minors open accounts?
A: Yes, minors can open with guardian consent or limited KYC; student friendly variants exist.
Q: What is joint account operation?
A: Joint accounts define operation instructions like either or survivor for flexibility and survivohip.
Q: What is a nominee in bank accounts?
A: A nominee is a peon designated to receive funds upon the account holders death.
Q: How do I add or change a nominee?
A: Submit a nomination form via branch or supported digital channels; keep details updated.
Q: What is account dormancy?
A: If no customer initiated transactions occur for 2 years, the account becomes dormant until reactivation steps are completed.
Q: How to reactivate a dormant account?
A: Complete re KYC and perform a customer initiated transaction per bank process.
Q: What is re KYC?
A: Periodic KYC updates based on risk category or profile changes to keep records current.
Q: Can I hold multiple savings accounts?
A: Yes, you can maintain multiple accounts across banks as per your needs.
Q: What is a family or bundled account?
A: Some banks offer linked family accounts or groups with consolidated benefits and fees.
Q: What is a womens/senior citizens savings account?
A: Segment specific variants offering tailored benefits like fee waivers or higher interest.
Q: What is a digital/insta savings account?
A: Fully online, quick onboarding accounts, often with eKYC and app fit servicing.
Q: What is a premium banking relationship?
A: Higher tier accounts offering concierge benefits, fee waivers, and relationship managers.
Q: What is a cheque book facility?
A: A booklet of cheques for payments; issuance depends on account type and request.
Q: What is CTS?
A: Cheque Truncation System that clears cheques from images for faster processing.
Q: What is passbook vs e statement?
A: Passbook is a physical transaction record; e statements are digital PDFs sent periodically.
Q: How do I download statements?
A: Use net banking or the mobile app to download PDF/CSV statements.
Q: What is a debit card?
A: A payment card linked to your account for ATM withdrawals, PoS, and e commerce transactions.
Q: How do I set card limits and controls?
A: Use the bank app to set domestic/international, contactless, online, and ATM usage limits.
Q: What is tokenization for cards?
A: Replacing the card number with a token for safer online transactions and storage.
Q: What is 2FA in banking apps?
A: Two factor authentication like OTP plus device/biometric for secure access and payments.
Q: How to update mobile number or email?
A: Use app/online flow or visit the branch with ID verification as required.
Q: Can I link UPI to my savings account?
A: Yes, you can link multiple bank accounts to UPI apps for instant payments.
Q: What is device binding?
A: Associating your specific phone/SIM with the bank/UPI app to prevent unauthorized access.
Q: What is a sweep account/sweep in FD?
A: Surplus savings balances auto move to FD and sweep back to maintain liquidity.
Q: What is account portability?
A: You can close and reopen at another bank; full portability across banks isnt mandated.
Q: What is lien marking on deposits?
A: A hold placed on your FD/amounts as security for loans or mandates.
Q: What is an overdraft (OD) against account?
A: A sanctioned limit allowing negative balances with interest charged on usage.
Q: What is an OD against FD?
A: Borrowing against FD at a spread over the FD rate without breaking the deposit.
Q: What is average monthly balance (AMB)?
A: The required average balance across the month to avoid non maintenance charges.
Q: What happens if I dont maintain AMB?
A: Banks may levy non maintenance charges as per the schedule.
Q: What is a zero balance savings account?
A: Accounts with no minimum balance requirement, like BSBD or salary accounts.
Q: What are account service charges?
A: Published fees for services beyond free limits; banks must disclose and update publicly.
Q: What is a locker account/relationship?
A: Agreement based safe deposit locker facility with defined responsibilities and charges.
Q: Can NRIs open savings accounts?
A: NRIs open NRE/NRO savings; resident savings accounts must be converted upon status change.
Q: What is repatriation for NRE/NRO?
A: NRE is fully repatriable; NRO repatriation is limited and subject to tax/documentation.
Q: What is RFC account?
A: Resident Foreign Currency account for returning NRIs to hold foreign currency balances.
Q: Can I have both NRE and NRO accounts?
A: Yes; use NRE for foreign income and NRO for Indian income flows.
Q: What is an account aggregator consent?
A: Your authorization to share account data securely with a licensed AA for specific purposes.
Q: Can I export transactions for taxes?
A: Yes, download CSV/PDF statements for reconciliation and filings.
Q: What is a service request?
A: Any account change instruction like address update, cheque book, or card reissue via app/branch.
Q: What is address proof update?
A: Submitting valid OVDs or accepted documents to change your address in bank records.
Q: What is identity proof update?
A: Providing updated ID documents when they expire or details change.
Q: What is PAN seeding?
A: Linking PAN to your account for compliance and TDS reporting.
Q: What is Aadhaar seeding?
A: Linking Aadhaar for KYC or subsidy benefits where applicable and with consent.
Q: How to enable e statements?
A: Opt in through app/net banking to receive periodic statements by email.
Q: What are account alerts?
A: SMS/app/email notifications for transactions, logins, and security events.
Q: How to secure my account?
A: Use strong passwords, enable biometrics/2FA, review app permissions, and avoid public Wi Fi.
Q: What is a transaction limit?
A: Per transaction and daily limits you can set for transfers and card usage.
Q: How to add a beneficiary?
A: Add via app/net banking; a cooling period may apply before large transfers.
Q: What is a scheduled transfer?
A: A future dated or recurring payment set in app/net banking for bills and savings.
Q: What is e mandate?
A: Digital authorization for recurring debits (EMIs/bills) via NACH/UPI with consent.
Q: What is account freezing?
A: Temporary restrictions due to KYC issues, suspicious activity, or regulatory orders until resolved.
Q: How to close my account?
A: Place a closure request, settle dues, and provide instructions for remaining balance payout.
Q: What is survivohip in joint accounts?
A: The surviving holder can operate or claim funds based on mandate after the others demise.
Q: What is a mandate holder?
A: A third party authorized to operate an account within specified limits by the account holder.
Q: What is standing instruction (SI)?
A: Automated periodic transfers between your accounts or billers.
Q: What is cheque return?
A: A cheque is returned unpaid due to reasons like insufficient funds or signature mismatch.
Q: What is stop payment?
A: An instruction to prevent payment of a cheque before it is cleared.
Q: What is account number masking?
A: Displaying only partial digits in communications to protect privacy.
Q: What is e KYC vs Video KYC?
A: e KYC uses Aadhaar OTP/XML; Video KYC is a live interaction with liveness and geo checks.
Q: Can I convert a savings account to NRO on becoming NRI?
A: Yes, resident accounts must be redesignated to NRO/NRE per status change.
Q: What is a family nomination split?
A: Some banks allow multiple nominees with assigned share ratios.
Q: What happens on account holders demise?
A: Funds are released to nominee/survivor or legal heir per due process and documents.
Q: What is cheque truncation image quality?
A: Cheques must meet image standards (MICR clarity) for CTS processing.
Q: What are inward vs outward cheques?
A: Inward are cheques deposited to your account; outward are cheques you issue to others.
Q: What is an instant virtual debit card?
A: A card number issued instantly in app for online transactions pending physical card delivery.
Q: Can I disable online or contactless payments?
A: Yes, toggle channels in card controls for security.
Q: What is an account package/plan?
A: Bundled benefits and fee structure under a specific account plan (e.g., premium, student).
Q: What is a salary account conveion?
A: If salary credits stop, banks may convert to a regular savings variant with applicable AMB.
Q: What is a bank identifier code on statements?
A: Codes like IFSC and branch details used for transfers and reconciliation.
Q: How are service charges notified?
A: Banks update websites/apps and may notify customers prior to changes.
Q: What is an account relationship number?
A: A unique ID for your overall relationship used in service and support.
Q: What is cheque book reissue?
A: Requesting new cheques upon exhaustion/loss; charges may apply as per schedule.
Q: What is an e receipt?
A: A digital confirmation of transactions, often with reference/UTR for proof.
Q: How to verify a payee before transfer?
A: Use name preview/confirmations in NEFT/IMPS/UPI and verify details carefully.
Q: What if I sent money to a wrong account?
A: Recovery isnt guaranteed; immediately inform bank and recipient bank to attempt recall.
Q: What is account hygiene?
A: Regularly reviewing transactions, updating KYC, and pruning unused channels/mandates.
Q: What is branchless banking?
A: Banking through BCs and digital channels without visiting branches.
Q: What is a relationship manager (RM)?
A: A dedicated advisor provided to premium/priority customers for service and products.
Q: Can I open FD/RD from my savings account online?
A: Yes, most banks allow instant FD/RD booking and linkage from savings.
Q: What is statement cycle preference?
A: Selecting monthly/quarterly e statement frequency or on demand download.
Q: How to raise grievances?
A: Use in app/branch channels; escalate unresolved issues to the Ombudsman.
Q: Whats the safest way to operate accounts?
A: Use official apps, enable 2FA and alerts, set transaction/card limits, and verify beneficiaries each time.
